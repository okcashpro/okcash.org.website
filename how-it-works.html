OK Basics for New Users


As a new user, you can get started with Okcash without needing to understand all the technical details. Once you have installed a web3 wallet like metamask, trustwallet or coinbase wallet on your computer, mobile phone, or IoT device, it will generate your first address, and you can create more addresses whenever needed. You can share your addresses with friends or customers so they can pay you or vice versa. This is similar to how email works, except that Okcash addresses should only be used once for enhanced privacy.





Balances - OK Blockchain and Multi-chain Ecosystem


The different blockchains serves as a shared public ledger on which the entire Okcash network relies. All confirmed transactions are recorded on the different chains, enabling Okcash wallets to calculate their spendable balances and verify that new transactions are using OK tokens legitimately held by the spender. With Okcash's multi-chain expansion, balances can now be maintained and accessed across multiple networks, including Ethereum, Binance Smart Chain, Polygon, Avalanche, Arbitrum, Optimism, Fantom, Base, and Solana. The integrity and chronological order of transactions are enforced through cryptography, ensuring security across all chains.



Transactions - Private Keys and Security


A transaction is a transfer of OK value between wallets that is recorded on the different chains. The wallets maintain a private piece of data called a private key or seed, which is used to sign transactions, proving they are authorized by the wallet owner. This signature also ensures that transactions cannot be altered once they are issued. Transactions are broadcast across the network of users and generally start receiving confirmations within seconds, particularly when staked in multi-chain environments such as BASE, Binance Smart Chain, or Polygon, where fast processing is optimized.



Staking - Multi-chain Staking Pools


Staking is a crucial component of the Okcash network and plays a key role in maintaining the security and integrity of the multi-chain ecosystem. Rather than staking on a single chain, Okcash now offers multi-chain staking pools across several supported networks, including BASE, Binance Smart Chain, Polygon, Avalanche, and Arbitrum. These staking pools are managed by Team.Finance, which provides additional security through triple-audited smart contracts.


By staking OK tokens in these pools, users participate in a distributed consensus system that confirms transactions across the different networks. Each staking pool provides significant Annual Percentage Yields (APY), encouraging users to support the networks while earning rewards. To join a staking pool, users simply connect their wallet (e.g., Trust Wallet, MetaMask, or Coinbase Wallet) to the pool on their preferred network, allowing them to earn based on their staked amount. Staking pools offer both stability for the Okcash network and rewarding opportunities for participants.



Processing - Decentralized Consensus Across Chains


Okcash employs a distributed consensus mechanism to confirm transactions across all supported chains, securing the network's neutrality and decentralized nature. Transactions are organized into blocks that follow strict cryptographic rules, preventing any alteration of prior blocks and ensuring that no single user or entity can control the blockchains. By diversifying across multiple chains, Okcash creates a resilient and secure multi-chain environment that aligns with the broader goals of decentralized finance (DeFi).
